Origin

The Osmanya script, invented in 1920-22 by Osman Yusuf Kenadid for the Somali language, consists of 22 consonants and 10 vowels. It's written from left to right, intended as an easy-to-learn alternative to Arabic and Latin scripts. Despite limited adoption, it holds historical and cultural significance.

Key Points

  • Created by Osman Yusuf Kenadid in 1920-22 for writing the Somali language.
  • An alphabetic script where each character represents a phoneme.
  • Written from left to right.
  • The Osmanya script consists of 22 consonants and 10 vowels.
  • Each consonant and vowel has two forms: one for the beginning or middle of a word, and one for the end of a word.
  • Unlike some scripts, Osmanya does not use conjunct forms; each letter is written separately.
  • Osmanya script does not have separate upper case and lower case letters
  • Although it gained some popularity during its creation, it was never adopted as the official script for Somali.
